CS Final Project

Hypothesis

  1. Is it profitable to bet on home teams, that are underdogs, to win the game outright?
  2. Is it profitable to bet on home teams, that are underdogs, to win the game outright, if the home team was on a ‘bye’ the week before?

Where I Gathered My Data

I gathered my data from: College Football Data Exporter.

I queried it by year, which provided me with a .csv file of every gambling line of the regular season from that year.

Data Cleaning and Integrity

After combining every year into one database, I had a total of 33,780 rows of data. This felt like a lot of games for an ~11-year time span, so I checked for duplicates in my data. This revealed ~20,000 duplicate lines, as the data source logged betting lines for the same games from different providers (e.g., FanDuel, DraftKings, MGM, etc.).

I resolved this issue by creating a new table called unique_lines: [unique_lines.sql].

This provided insights into profitable trends. I then created a query to find winners, losers, total games, Moneyline values, and profitability for spreads from 0.5–10.5: [cfb_underdogs_query.sql].

Effect of Bye Weeks

To test if teams coming off a bye week were more profitable, I ran the same test while cross-referencing team schedules: [cfb_underdogs_bye_query.sql].

The results were surprisingly underwhelming compared to the profitability of regular home underdogs.
